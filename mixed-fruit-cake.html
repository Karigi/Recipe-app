Mixed Fruit Cake

Ingridients

  • 1 and 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup brown sugar, packed
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 and 1/2 cups mixed dried fruits (raisins, currants, sultanas, chopped dried apricots)
  • 1/2 cup chopped nuts (walnuts, almonds, cashews)
  • 1/2 cup candied ginger (optional)
  • 1/4 cup dark rum or fruit juice (optional, for soaking)
  • 1/4 cup glacé cherries (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest
  • 1 tablespoon finely grated orange zest

Preparation Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C). Grease and line a 9-inch round cake pan or a loaf pan with parchment paper.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking soda, baking powder, spices (cinnamon, nutmeg, cloves), and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, cream together the softened butter and brown sugar until light and fluffy using a hand mixer or stand mixer.
  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Gradually add the flour mixture to the wet ingredients, alternating with the orange juice and milk. Mix until just combined.
  6. Fold in the dried fruits, chopped nuts, glacé cherries, candied ginger, lemon zest, and orange zest. Stir gently until evenly distributed.
  7. If using dark rum or fruit juice, soak the dried fruits in it for about 30 minutes before adding them to the batter for extra flavor.
  8. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and spread it evenly.
  9. Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  10. Allow the cake to cool in the pan for about 10 minutes, then transfer it to a wire rack to cool completely.
  11. Once completely cooled, the fruit cake can be served as is, or optionally brushed with a little more rum or fruit juice for added moisture and flavor.
  12. Store the cake in an airtight container for up to a week, or refrigerate for longer shelf life.
